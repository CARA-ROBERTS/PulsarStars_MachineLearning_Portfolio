Statistics from the Original Dataset for Neural Net Model Predictions

The remaning dataset showcases the the data parameters throughout the original dataset. The saved neural (from the previous page) net model will predit Pulsar Stars based on each parameter.

**Given the DataFrame results and Chart visualiztion, assumption of the predictive result of a Pulsar Star will likely occur the greater the numerical value of the star data observations. Further mock datasets and prediction needed to validate this assumption.**

Building a Mock Dataset

In order build a true mock dataset to predict accurately from the saved Neural Net Model, the Max and Min params were pulled form the original dataset.

Setting random data between the max/min params will ensure realistic and probable star data observation. This mock dataset represents possible star observation to predict real viability of determining Pulsar Stars.

Mock Dataset For Neural Net Model Predictions

The saved neural net model is utilized on the Mock DataFrame with function model.predict_classes(mockDF[:]). This function predicts Pulsar Star outcomes on each observational row of star data.

The Pulsar Stars predictions has mixed results across mock stars observation data. Although the Mock DataFrame has only 10 star observations and preditive results, the variation of the star data and mixed Pulsar predictions gives perspective that the greater the numerical value has no relevence to whether the predict result is a Pulsar Star or not.

A double axis bar and line chart visualizes the prediction results based on the Pulsar Star Mock DataFrame. The blue bar represents Pulsar star prediction and color line is classifiers value data.
