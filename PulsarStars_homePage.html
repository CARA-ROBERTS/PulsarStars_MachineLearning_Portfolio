Predicting Pulsar Stars with Machine Learning

What are Pulsar Stars?

Pulsars are a type of neutron star. It is a dense, highly magnetized, and rapidly rotating star that emits electromagnetic radiation from its magnetic poles. The star's radiation can only be detected when the radiation beam is focused towards Earth. The rapid rotation of the pulsar star emits the beam towards Earth in a pulse like method. The star has a precise, small rotation that emits a beam towards Earth in a pulse of milliseconds to seconds.

***Diagram of a Pulsar Star, a Neutron Star becomes a Pulsar when it emits radiation beam from its magnetic poles.

***As visualized, Pulsar Stars are at the end of the Star Life Cylce. Pulsars are remnants of supermassive stars.

How can they be predicted?

Pulsar Stars can be determined through different series of Machine Learning methods. Previous star observations from a csv contain data with specific parameters relative to predict Pulsar and non-Pulsar stars. Through the following pages, two methods of Machine Learning showcase how to predict Pulsars utilizing Python models: a Random Forest and a Neural Network. Python libriaries SKLearn, Tensorflow.keras, and Random Forest Classifier were intrumental to developing the predict models.
